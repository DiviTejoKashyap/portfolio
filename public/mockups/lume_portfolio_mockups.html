Bidirectional somatosensory feedback via transcranial arrays

AUTHORS: CHEN, L., WANG, J., ET AL. // PUBLISHED: JAN 2026

Current non-invasive brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) primarily focus on motor decoding. However, closing the sensorimotor loop requires high-resolution haptic feedback that mimics natural mechanoreception. Without this feedback, users experience significant cognitive load when attempting precise distal object manipulation.

Here we introduce a novel grid paradigm leveraging focused transcranial neuromodulation. By multiplexing signals across a 256-channel high-density array, we bypass the spatial limitations of traditional tFUS (transcranial focused ultrasound). Our approach utilizes constructive interference to create focal points as small as 2mm³ within the somatosensory cortex (S1).

The primary limitation observed during phase II trials was the refractory period synchronization between adjacent cortical columns. If the stimulation pulse width exceeded 40ms, subjects consistently reported diffuse parasthesia rather than localized, distinct tactile sensations. This suggests a hard biological limit on temporal resolution using continuous wave methodologies.
